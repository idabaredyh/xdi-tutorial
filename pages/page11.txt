<h1>Step 11: XDI/JSON</h1>

<p>Another way of serializing an XDI graph is the <span class="term">XDI/JSON</span> format.</p>

<p class="indent">
<span class="xri">
{<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;"/": [<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"@acme"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;],<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;"=alice/+friend": [<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"=bob"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;],<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;"=alice<+email>&/&": "alice@email.com"<br>
}<br>
</span>
</p>

<p>The two formats are equivalent in terms of the XDI graphs they can describe.</p>
